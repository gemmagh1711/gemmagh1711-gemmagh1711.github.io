Teaching Pedagogy and Philosophy


Pedagogy

As a pre-service teacher, my pedagogy emphasizes the importance of creating an inclusive and engaging learning environment where every student feels valued and supported. I believe in using a variety of instructional strategies that cater to diverse learning styles, ensuring that all students have the opportunity to succeed.

My approach is student-centered, focusing on active learning, collaboration, and the integration of real-world experiences into the classroom. Using formative assessments to guide instruction and to provide timely feedback that helps students grow academically and personally is important to me. By continuously reflecting on my teaching practices, I strive to create lessons that are both challenging and accessible, encouraging all students to reach their highest potential. This will ensure all individually diverse students are catered to, to give them equal opportunity to thrive.

Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is grounded in the belief that education is a fundamental right and a fundamental step for positive change in individuals and society. Teaching is a vocation that goes beyond passing on knowledge; it involves supporting critical thinking, creativity, and a lifelong love of learning.

Every student has unique strengths and potential, and it is my responsibility to create an environment that fosters that uniqueness and allows for growth, curiosity, and resilience. Inclusivity, respect, and empathy are at the heart of my educational values. These values guide me to create a classroom where all students feel safe, respected, and motivated to learn. I am committed to continuous learning and reflective practice, continually seeking to improve my teaching allowing me to better serve my students.
